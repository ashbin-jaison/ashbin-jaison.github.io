Background

About Me

I am a climate risk modeler with a PhD in applied statistics from the University of Bergen, specializing in extreme event modelling, catastrophe risk assessment, and climate-related financial risk. My work sits at the intersection of climate science, statistical modelling, and natural catastrophe risk.

My doctoral research at the Bjerknes Centre for Climate Research focused on developing probabilistic models that link extreme meteorological hazards to observed societal impacts — transforming windstorm forecasts into quantified estimates of infrastructure losses across Norwegian municipalities.

I have extensive experience processing large-scale gridded datasets (NORA3, ERA5, EURO-CORDEX) using Python-based scientific computing pipelines on HPC infrastructure, and applying machine learning methods to climate risk problems.

Beyond academia, I have applied similar modelling approaches in maritime catastrophe risk analysis, including probabilistic modelling of extreme sea states using bivariate distributions and global reanalysis datasets.
